## 3. 500-word investor / accelerator version

`YNX is an early open-source infrastructure project focused on AI-native
execution, policy-bounded onchain actions, and developer-facing Web4 tooling.
The project is strongest today as infrastructure rather than as a token story,
and its credibility comes from public artifacts rather than presentation
framing. YNX already has a public website, a public repository, public
technical and diligence materials, and live public-testnet evidence across RPC,
indexer, explorer, AI gateway, and Web4 surfaces.

The core problem YNX is addressing is that early crypto and agentic systems are
often hard to inspect, hard to integrate, and described more aggressively than
their actual state of readiness. Builders need infrastructure that is easier to
verify, safer to work with, and more disciplined about execution boundaries.
YNX is being built to provide that layer through policy controls, session
authorization, AI settlement rails, and builder-facing infrastructure.

The honest boundary matters. YNX should not be described as a production-grade
mainnet financial network, a custody platform, or a fully formed operating
company. No public legal entity is announced yet, no external audit report is
published yet, and no external legal memo is published yet. For that reason,
the best current fit is grants, sponsorships, strategic infrastructure support,
and only exploratory early-stage investor conversations that understand company
formation and legal ownership are still open work items.

What makes YNX worth engaging now is that the project is already beyond concept
stage. The technical possibility has been demonstrated in public. The next step
is to turn early infrastructure into something stronger on product packaging,
operational maturity, ecosystem fit, and company readiness. Funding would be
used for security review and remediation, observability and disaster recovery,
builder tooling and documentation, external operator onboarding, and legal and
policy implementation.

The right way to underwrite YNX today is not as a valuation-premium chain
story. It is as an early infrastructure project with real public evidence,
honest disclosure, and a credible path to becoming a more durable company if
the technical base is hardened and adoption proof points are added.`

## 4. Standard use-of-funds breakdown

Use this structure consistently:

- security review, remediation, and incident-response hardening;
- observability, alerting, backups, and disaster recovery;
- builder tooling, SDKs, docs, and integration workflows;
- validator diversity and external operator onboarding;
- legal structuring, terms, privacy, and compliance implementation;
- selective pilot customer or ecosystem partner onboarding.

## 5. Current boundary language

Use these exact distinctions:

- `live` means live on public testnet unless explicitly stated otherwise.
- `public testnet` is the canonical operating description today.
- `tradable` should be used only for public-testnet scope unless the asset and
  venue are named and verifiable.
- `wrapped asset` refers to testnet representations unless explicitly stated
  otherwise.
- `full-loop-tested` is testnet evidence, not production safety.
- `automatic-loop-ready` is a testnet configuration state, not a production
  claim.
- `production` should be avoided unless the exact subsystem is named and the
  production boundary is defensible.
- `mainnet-candidate` means gated future work, not current state.
- `decentralization` should be described conservatively; validator
  independence is still improving.
- `non-custodial` is the intended operating boundary and should not be used to
  imply every external route is legally complete.
- `settlement` refers to technical lifecycle rails, not licensed financial
  settlement finality.
- `compliance-ready` should be avoided as a global label; only name specific
  policy or documentation pieces that exist.

## 6. Direct answers to common questions

### What is the strongest proof today?

`The strongest proof is real public technical work: live public-testnet
endpoints, public code, public documentation, and observable infrastructure
evidence.`

### What is YNX not claiming?

`YNX is not claiming formed-company status, production-grade mainnet custody,
completed external audit coverage, or completed legal sign-off.`

### What barriers are still open?

`The main remaining barriers are company formation, legal ownership and policy
completion, external audit coverage, stronger validator independence, and
commercial adoption proof.`

### What is this round actually funding?

`This round is funding hardening and organization of an already public
technical stack: security, operations, developer tooling, documentation,
ecosystem packaging, and company-readiness basics.`

### Why engage now?

`Because YNX already has public technical evidence, but has not yet closed the
gap between technical execution and company-grade maturity. Early support can
still be high-leverage.`
