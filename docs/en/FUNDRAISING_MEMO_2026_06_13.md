# YNX Fundraising Memo

Status: investor diligence draft  
Last updated: 2026-06-14  
Canonical language: English

## 1. One-Line Summary

YNX is a live public-testnet Web4 execution layer for humans and AI agents,
focused on policy-bounded onchain actions, fast EVM-compatible execution, and
verifiable AI settlement infrastructure.

## 2. What Exists Today

As of June 13, 2026, YNX already has:

- live public RPC, EVM RPC, REST, faucet, indexer, explorer, AI gateway, and
  Web4 hub endpoints;
- 4 bonded validators on the public testnet;
- working owner-policy-session authorization flows for protected agent actions;
- live AI settlement rails with vault/job/result/finalize workflow;
- public bridge infrastructure with `5/5` full-loop-tested testnet routes and
  only `2/5` routes with the strongest publicly observed automatic evidence;
- operator scripts, probes, runbooks, and public readiness evidence.

YNX is therefore past the "concept deck" stage. The current task is not proving
technical possibility. The current task is hardening, narrowing product scope,
and turning live infrastructure into an investable and commercially legible
company.

Current diligence-visible blockers still open:

- no external security audit report is included yet;
- no external legal memo is included yet;
- website legal disclosure routes such as `/privacy` and `/terms` are not yet
  published as standalone pages as of June 14, 2026;
- company formation, named responsible owners, and dedicated security/contact
  aliases are still listed as readiness work, not completed facts.

## 3. Investment Thesis

YNX sits at the intersection of four durable needs:

1. AI agents need bounded execution, not just wallet signing.
2. Onchain applications need low-friction EVM access with strong operator
   controls.
3. Machine payments and task settlement need auditable lifecycle rails.
4. Infrastructure businesses need revenue models that do not depend on token
   speculation.

The project is strongest when positioned as a non-custodial infrastructure
company with protocol leverage, not as a speculative token story.

What this memo should not imply:

- not a novel-consensus moat story yet;
- not a mainnet-ready financial-infrastructure claim;
- not a production custody or production bridge claim.

## 4. Why Now

The market is moving from "AI can suggest" to "AI can act". That shift creates
new demand for:

- policy enforcement before execution;
- auditable session scopes;
- machine-budget controls;
- task/result/payment settlement rails;
- safer operator workflows for AI-assisted execution.

YNX already has these primitives live on a public testnet. That gives the team
something many early-stage protocol projects do not have: evidence.

The strongest evidence is at the execution and policy layer, not at the generic
L1-construction layer.

## 4.5 Why Keep A Chain Form At All

The honest answer is not "because consensus itself is the moat."

The honest answer is:

- YNX wants policy, session scope, execution, and settlement evidence to live
  on one verifiable execution surface;
- YNX benefits from controlling the integrated developer/operator stack;
- if the market only values the middleware layer, YNX should keep describing
  itself as an execution-infrastructure company first.

That means the chain thesis is still being earned through adoption, not assumed
as a free valuation premium.

## 5. Product Scope To Fund

The company should fund the following core scope first:

- Web4 policy and session infrastructure;
- AI settlement and machine-payment rails;
- hosted RPC / indexer / explorer / gateway infrastructure;
- builder tooling, SDKs, and operator observability;
- limited bridge and wrapped-asset support where it directly helps developer
  workflows, while keeping all external language testnet-scoped.

De-prioritized scope:

- any fundraising narrative that depends on BNB Chain support;
- any claim that BSC route completion is required to make the company fundable;
- any marketing that implies production custody or live mainnet external asset
  redemption.

The BSC/BNB route may remain as optional technical backlog, but it should not
be a dependency for the fundraising story, company formation, or near-term use
of funds.

## 6. Use Of Funds

The next financing round should primarily fund:

- security review, remediation, and incident-response hardening;
- production observability, alerting, backups, and disaster recovery;
- developer experience, docs, and SDK maturation;
- validator diversity and external operator onboarding;
- legal structuring, terms, privacy, and compliance implementation;
- pilot customer onboarding for hosted infra and enterprise Web4 / AI use cases.

## 7. Revenue Model

YNX should be underwritten by non-speculative revenue lines:

- hosted API and infrastructure subscriptions;
- private or dedicated deployments;
- SLA and enterprise support contracts;
- validator, monitoring, and disaster-recovery tooling;
- AI execution infrastructure and machine-payment workflows;
- audit, integration, and certification services for ecosystem projects.

NYXT should remain a utility and coordination layer, not the only survival
assumption of the company.

## 8. What Investors Should Understand Clearly

- YNX is live on public testnet, not yet a mainnet-grade production network.
- The strongest moat today is the Web4 policy/session and AI settlement stack,
  not generic Cosmos/EVM assembly.
- EVM compatibility is onboarding leverage, not differentiation by itself.
- Validator decentralization is improving, but not yet strong enough for an
  aggressive decentralization claim.
- The business should stay non-custodial by default.
- Wrapped testnet routes are evidence of architecture, not proof of licensed
  production custody.
- Automatic-loop-ready is a testnet configuration state, not a synonym for
  production safety or repeated scale proof.
- Historical tokenomics drafts exist, but they are not the current financing
  term sheet or a founder-fee commitment.
- The best financing use case is infrastructure expansion and hardening, not
  speculative liquidity theater.

## 9. The Ask

YNX is suitable for:

- pre-seed / seed infrastructure investors;
- grant programs that support open protocol tooling, safe agent execution, or
  public developer infrastructure;
- strategic partners that care about AI action safety, machine payments, or
  enterprise Web3/Web4 rails.

Recommended framing:

`We are raising to harden and commercialize a live Web4 and AI-execution
infrastructure stack, not to fund a paper architecture.`
