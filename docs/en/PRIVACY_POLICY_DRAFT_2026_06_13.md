# YNX Privacy Policy Draft

Status: draft  
Last updated: 2026-06-13  
Canonical language: English

## 1. Purpose

This is a working privacy policy draft for YNX public website, hosted APIs,
developer services, support workflows, and related infrastructure.

## 2. Data We May Collect

Depending on the service, YNX may collect:

- contact information such as name and email;
- account and authentication metadata;
- API usage logs;
- support and communications records;
- device, browser, and network metadata;
- wallet addresses or onchain identifiers when users connect Web3 tools;
- analytics and operational telemetry.

## 3. Why We Collect Data

Data may be processed to:

- operate public services;
- authenticate users and secure sessions;
- monitor abuse, fraud, and incidents;
- provide support and service notices;
- analyze reliability and improve products;
- meet contractual or legal obligations.

## 4. Data Sharing

YNX may share data with:

- cloud and infrastructure providers;
- analytics, monitoring, and security vendors;
- professional advisers and auditors;
- authorities where legally required.

YNX should not sell personal data as a default business model.

## 5. Data Retention

YNX should keep data only as long as reasonably necessary for operations,
security, legal compliance, and dispute handling. Retention schedules should be
formalized internally.

## 6. Security

YNX should use reasonable technical and organizational safeguards, but no
system can be guaranteed to be perfectly secure.

## 7. International Transfers

Because YNX infrastructure may rely on cloud and service providers across
jurisdictions, data may be processed outside the user's home jurisdiction.

## 8. User Rights

Where applicable law provides access, correction, deletion, withdrawal of
consent, or related privacy rights, YNX should provide a contact channel for
such requests.

## 9. Testnet Notice

Testnet activity may still generate logs, support records, and telemetry even
though the assets themselves are non-production.

## 10. Contact And DPO

The final published version should include:

- privacy contact email;
- Data Protection Officer contact;
- entity name;
- jurisdiction-specific rights wording after counsel review.
