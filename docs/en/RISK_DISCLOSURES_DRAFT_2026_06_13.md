# YNX Risk Disclosures Draft

Status: draft  
Last updated: 2026-06-13  
Canonical language: English

## Core Risk Statement

YNX is currently a public-testnet infrastructure project. It is not appropriate
to present testnet functionality as audited production custody, guaranteed
financial infrastructure, or investment-grade asset rails.

## 1. Testnet Risk

Services may reset, fail, fork, rate-limit, or change without notice. Testnet
transactions and assets may have no production value.

## 2. Smart Contract And Software Risk

Bugs, vulnerabilities, integration failures, signing issues, or dependency
failures may cause outages or loss of testnet functionality.

## 3. Bridge And External Network Risk

Wrapped testnet routes depend on external chains, watchers, signers, lockboxes,
or operator workflows. These systems may fail or remain unsuitable for
production until separately audited and governed.

## 4. AI And Automation Risk

AI outputs, preflight checks, and agent-driven actions may be incorrect,
incomplete, delayed, or unsafe if misconfigured. Human review and policy
controls remain important.

## 5. Regulatory Risk

Rules applicable to tokens, hosted infrastructure, digital-token services,
privacy, sanctions, or AI systems may change. Product scope may need to be
limited or redesigned based on legal review.

## 6. No Yield / No Profit Promise

YNX, NYXT, and any testnet asset should not be marketed as guaranteed return,
yield, or profit opportunities.

## 7. Production Gap Risk

Public testnet evidence is useful, but it is not the same as:

- external security audit completion;
- licensed regulated operations;
- production incident readiness;
- deep liquidity;
- production custody or redemption.

## 8. Counterparty And Infrastructure Risk

Cloud vendors, upstream providers, validator operators, and external
integrations may fail or underperform.

## 9. Founder Disclosure Rule

Founders, team members, and partners should use precise language. They should
not overstate decentralization, security guarantees, liquidity, or regulatory
status.
