# YNX Chain Web4 Charter

This document defines the next-generation YNX Chain direction. It treats Web4 as a full digital execution network, not as a Web3 interface with an AI chatbot attached.

## 1. Positioning

YNX Chain should be positioned as:

> An AI-native resource economy chain for global users, developers, merchants, AI agents, and institutions.

The goal is not merely to build another fast chain. The goal is to build a global execution layer that combines:

- Ethereum-grade developer trust;
- Solana-class throughput and low-latency user experience;
- TRON-style resource ownership and low-friction payments;
- Base-style distribution and compliance readiness;
- Hyperliquid-style focus on high-value revenue flows;
- native AI operations, accountable asset movement, and protected evidence workflows.

The public product name should be `YNX Chain`. `V3` should remain an internal migration label only.

## 2. Web4 Definition

YNX defines Web4 as:

> A digital society operating layer that upgrades Web3 asset ownership and smart contracts with AI agents, resource accounts, verifiable identity, automated execution, evidence retention, and dispute workflows.

Web3 says: I own my account and assets.

YNX Web4 says:

- I own my assets;
- I own my identity;
- I own my AI agents;
- I own allocatable network resources;
- I can authorize AI to act within policy;
- I can audit every authorization and fund movement;
- I can use the same trusted account across finance, commerce, development, entertainment, governance, and disputes.

## 4. NYXT Role

NYXT should not be positioned around scheduled burns. It should be positioned as the resource, governance, and coordination asset of YNX Chain.

NYXT should anchor four resource classes:

| Resource | Use | User Experience |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bandwidth | transfers, chat, messages, light transactions | low-cost daily actions |
| Compute | smart contracts, DEX, bridge, complex transactions | priced high-load execution |
| AI Credits | inference, agent execution, chain tasks, automation | AI capacity like cloud credits |
| Trust Credits | tracing, risk, evidence, compliance, arbitration | priced security and institutional workflows |

Users can hold or stake NYXT to receive resources. Merchants, wallets, dApps, and institutions can sponsor resources so end users experience low-friction or gasless flows.

## 5. Revenue Model

YNX Chain should combine protocol revenue and company service revenue.

Protocol revenue:

- base transaction fees;
- resource rental fees;
- AI execution fees;
- Trust / Trace report fees;
- bridge route fees;
- merchant settlement fees;
- verification and proof fees;
- dispute and evidence workflow fees.

Company service revenue:

- official RPC, indexer, and explorer APIs;
- IDE cloud services;
- AI Gateway subscriptions;
- enterprise Web4 deployments;
- merchant payment dashboards;
- compliance and risk reports;
- official validator and infrastructure provider services;
- ecosystem support and integrations.

Founder and team economics should be public, long-term, and explainable through vesting, development funds, company service revenue, official infrastructure revenue, and capped ecosystem management fees. Hidden extraction should not be part of the model.

YNX Chain should ship as an integrated system:

- Chain: consensus, execution, resource model, accounts, EVM compatibility;
- Explorer: blocks, transactions, accounts, resources, AI jobs, trace graphs;
- Wallet: resource account, chat, payment, authorization, risk warnings;
- AI: embedded assistant and policy-bounded agent execution;
- IDE: build, compile, deploy, verify, debug with AI;
- Bridge: cross-chain assets, route readiness, risk and evidence;
- Payments: stablecoins, merchants, subscriptions, invoices, refunds, card flows;
- Governance: validators, proposals, treasury, grants;
- Trace: lot lineage, pro-rata taint, case reports;
- Court / Dispute: evidence, mediation, arbitration, legal exports;
- Marketplace: apps, agents, data, resources, services;
- Entertainment: games, NFTs, tickets, creator economy.

## 9. Final Requirement

YNX Chain must be:

- simple, low-cost, safe, and AI-assisted for users;
- EVM-compatible and complete for developers;
- stablecoin-ready and dispute-aware for merchants;
- auditable and permissioned for institutions;
- budgeted, policy-bounded, and accountable for AI agents;
- economically sustainable for validators, the treasury, the ecosystem, and the founding team;
- globally compatible with wallets, exchanges, stablecoins, AI systems, developers, merchants, and legal workflows.

In one sentence:

> YNX Chain should become the Web4 execution network for the AI era: covering Web3 assets and contracts while expanding into AI, chat, development, entertainment, payments, commerce, evidence, and dispute resolution through a durable resource economy.
